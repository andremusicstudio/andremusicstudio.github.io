Studio Policy

Students Responsibilities

The student agrees to arrive at each lesson punctually, well prepared, and with all necessary materials. Student must take good care of the music instrument, keep their nails short, and hands clean.

Parents Responsibility

The Parents to provide an instrument in the best possible condition, a metronome, and a place allowing quiet and uninterrupted practice. Be helpful, interested and supportive. Parents are welcome to attend lessons at any time.

Teacher Responsibilities

The teacher will provide: -a positive, supporting and enthusiastic atmosphere for learning -information where to rent and how to care for your instrument -musical materials

Tuition

Tuition is due on, or before the first lesson of each month. Parents should calculate the amount of lessons in the upcoming month and make a payment. We accept cash or checks. Make checks payable to Galina Andre

Cancellations

Please think and plan ahead, and remember your lesson time. If you need to cancel a lesson, make every effort to give the teacher as much notice as possible, at least 24 hours. If it possible one can exchange lesson times with another student.

Arrival

Plan to arrive a couple minutes before your lesson. Please remove your shoes and leave them on the landing inside the front door. Allow enough time before your lesson to unpack your violin and wash your hands. Students who are late cannot be guaranteed a full lesson.

Teacher Cancellation

Lessons that I cancel for any reason will be rescheduled or credit will be given towards the next month's tuition.

Preparedness

Please bring all the materials that I have requested. If photo-copied music was given during the lesson, please keep it neatly in a folder so that it won't get lost or damaged. Long ﬁngernails prevent the development of good violin technique and make it impossible to play musically. I might ask students who arrive to the lesson with ﬁngernails that are too long to trim/ﬁle them at the lesson.
